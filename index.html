02 — Patterns

Cognitive Distortions

These are the six most common patterns the mind uses to distort reality. Recognising them is the first act of healing.

01
◼◻

All-or-Nothing

Black & White Thinking

Seeing situations in extreme binary terms. If it isn’t perfect, it’s a complete failure — no middle ground.

EXAMPLE

“I made one mistake — I’m completely incompetent.”

REFRAME

Most situations exist on a spectrum. One mistake doesn’t define your overall competence.

02
∞

Overgeneralization

Always / Never Thinking

Drawing sweeping conclusions from a single event. One negative experience becomes a permanent universal pattern.

EXAMPLE

“This always happens to me. I never get anything right.”

REFRAME

This happened once. It doesn’t define a permanent pattern about your life.

03
⚡

Catastrophizing

Worst-Case Thinking

Expecting the worst possible outcome and exaggerating the significance of every problem or setback.

EXAMPLE

“If I fail this, my entire career will be ruined.”

REFRAME

What’s the realistic outcome? Most setbacks are recoverable. You’ve handled difficulty before.

04
◎

Mind Reading

Assuming Others’ Thoughts

Believing you know what others are thinking — usually that they’re judging or thinking negatively of you.

EXAMPLE

“They didn’t reply — they must be angry with me.”

REFRAME

You cannot know what someone else is thinking. There are many possible reasons for their behaviour.

05
⊗

Should Statements

Rigid Internal Rules

Imposing rigid rules using “should,” “must,” or “ought to.” Violations create guilt or resentment that compounds.

EXAMPLE

“I should be further along. I must not feel this way.”

REFRAME

Replace “should” with “I would prefer.” Remove the judgment from the wish.

06
⬡

Labeling

Identity-Based Judgment

Attaching a global, negative identity label to yourself or others based on a single event or mistake.

EXAMPLE

“I forgot to reply — I’m such a terrible friend.”

REFRAME

You made one mistake. That’s an action, not an identity. Actions can change. You are not a label.
